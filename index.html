Noah Andrews

The World of

Charles & Ray Eames

12 January 2016

With a grand sense of adventure, Charles and Ray Eams turned their curiosity and boundless enthusiasm into creations that established them as a truly great husband and wife design team. Their unqiue synergy led to a whole new look into furniture. Lean and modern. Playful and functional. Sleek, sophisticated and beautifully simple. That was and is the "Eames look".

That look and their relationship with Herman Miller started with moulded plywood chairs in the late 1940s and included the world-renownded Eames lounge chair, now in the permanent collection of the Museum of Modern Art in New York

Charles and Ray achieved their monumental success by approaching each project the same way: Does it interest and intrigue us? Can we make it better?

They loved their work, which was a combination of art and science, design and architecture, process and product, style and function. "The details are not details", said Charles. "They make the product".

Their own concepts evolved over time, not overnight. As Charles noted about the development of the Moulded Plywood Chairs, "Yes, it was a flash of inspiration", he said, "a kind of a 30-year flash".

A design critic ounce said that this extraordinary couple "just wanted to make the world a better place". That they did. That also made it a lot more interesting.
